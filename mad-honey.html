Mad Honey: What Parents Should Know

Mad honey contains grayanotoxins from rhododendron nectar. Even small amounts can trigger vomiting, low heart rate, and fainting within minutes.

No proven benefits; hospitals have treated U.S. cases after online purchases. Seek emergency care and call Poison Control immediately.

References

  1. Gunduz A et al. Clin Toxicol (Phila). 2008;46(6):540‑544.
  2. CDC: Mad honey poisoning in travelers — MMWR (2023).
