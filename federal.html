Federal – How to Take Action

At the national level, the FDA tests and warns; the DEA schedules drugs; and Congress funds and closes loopholes. Here’s what to push for.

FDA — Food & Drug Administration

What FDA can do
• Test suspect “supplements” and publish parent-friendly warnings.
• Send warning letters, seize misbranded/adulterated products.
• Recommend scheduling of dangerous substances to the DEA.

Limitations
• Cannot make a drug illegal alone (DEA handles scheduling).
• Cannot police every gas station or website.

Ask FDA to
• Keep testing mood/energy “supplements”.
• Post clear alerts (Kratom, Tianeptine, Delta-8, Amanita, etc.).
• Formally recommend scheduling of kratom, tianeptine, phenibut.

DEA — Drug Enforcement Administration

What DEA can do
• Place substances into Schedule I (ban retail sales/possession).
• Investigate interstate shippers and online distributors.

Limitations
• Scheduling requires evidence and process; lobbying can slow it.

Ask DEA to
• Use emergency scheduling when harm is clear (e.g., tianeptine).
• Coordinate with states on parcel/online enforcement.

U.S. Senators & Representatives

What Congress can do
• Pass national bans and close supplement loopholes.
• Hold hearings to speed FDA/DEA action.
• Fund poison-control, hospital surveillance, and school education.

Limitations
• Federal laws take time and votes.

Ask Congress to
• Support bills banning gas-station drugs (kratom, tianeptine, phenibut, “mushroom” edibles).
• Fund data systems and outreach so parents get facts fast.
