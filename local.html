Local – How to Take Action

Cities and counties can protect neighborhoods today with ordinances, licensing, and community education.

City & County Councils

What they can do
• Ban sales inside city limits; require warning signs where “herbal supplements” are sold.
• Revoke or deny business licenses to retailers selling gas-station drugs.
• Hold public hearings to build community support.

Limitations
• State law can preempt local rules; enforcement resources vary.

Ask them to
• Pass local bans/resolutions urging state action.
• Fund compliance checks and merchant education.

Police & Local Law Enforcement

What they can do
• Enforce state/federal drug laws; inspect retailers.
• Collect evidence and coordinate with health departments.

Limitations
• Can act only when products are illegal or misbranded under law.

Ask them to
• Track outlets selling these products; support community meetings.
• Enforce bans once ordinances pass.

Teachers & Schools

What they can do
• Teach students/parents what gas-station drugs really are.
• Share state/local health alerts; watch for new products in schools.

Limitations
• Schools can’t control retail — only education and campus policy.

Ask them to
• Add this content to health classes.
• Send flyers to families; join local prevention coalitions.
