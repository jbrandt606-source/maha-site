THC Extracts: What Parents Need to Know

Delta-8, Delta-10, and THC-O are synthetically altered THC molecules sold in vape shops and gas stations. They behave like marijuana — and sometimes more dangerously.

“Hemp” label is deceptive. Delta-8 and Delta-10 are made via chemical conversions and produce psychoactive effects similar to cannabis — not benign “herbal” products.1–2

Child exposures are rising. Poison centers report increasing calls where children ingest gummies or cartridges mislabeled as THC-O or hemp products.3–5

Label errors are common. Independent lab tests find many products under- or overstate THC content by large margins and contain dangerous byproducts or residual solvents.2,6

THC-O is federally illegal. The DEA confirmed THC acetate (THC-O) is a Schedule I controlled substance, not hemp. Use might carry legal as well as health risk.7

Parent Playbook

What to watch for: Gummies, vape cartridges, pens, or packaged “hemp” edibles labeled “legal THC.” Flavors like candy, fruit, or dessert mask potency. Symptoms: euphoria, panic, hallucinations, fast heart rate, vomiting.3–5,6

If someone is severely intoxicated: Call 911. Maintain calm, prevent injury, and provide product packaging to medical staff. For help, call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222.

Paths of access: Vape/gas shops, online vendors, or social-media ads. Some products ship across state lines bypassing regulation.2,4

Talk about it—script: “Some ‘hemp’ products are strong THC analogs. It’s not safe to assume packaging is accurate. Let’s look up verified lab reports and talk through safer choices.”

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: “If it’s sold legally, it’s safe.”
Fact: Many products bypass regulation, leading to mislabeled doses and chemical impurities.2,6

Myth: “THC-O is benign because it’s ‘hemp-derived.’”
Fact: THC acetate (THC-O) is federally illegal and may have higher potency plus additional toxicity risks.
