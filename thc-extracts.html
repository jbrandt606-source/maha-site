THC Extracts: What Parents Should Know

Delta-8, Delta-10, and THC-O are lab-made THC products often sold as “hemp” at vape/gas shops. Effects can match or exceed marijuana — with more labeling and dosing problems.

“Hemp” ≠ harmless. Delta-8/Delta-10 are psychoactive cannabinoids created from CBD. The FDA and CDC report adverse events including vomiting, hallucinations, and psychosis.1–3

Kids get exposed. Poison-center data and pediatric reports show rising exposures from gummies and vapes with candy flavors/packaging.4–6

Potency & purity vary. Labels often misstate strength; products can contain byproducts/solvents from conversion chemistry. Doses vary widely from batch to batch.1–2

THC-O is illegal. DEA clarified THC-O (THC acetate) is a Schedule I controlled substance (not hemp). It may be more potent and made with hazardous reagents.7

Parent Playbook

What to watch for. Brightly colored gummies, “hemp” vapes, disposable pens, and “cereal-bar” edibles labeled Delta-8/Delta-10/THC-O. Symptoms: red eyes, anxiety or panic, fast heart rate, hallucinations, extreme drowsiness, vomiting.1–6

If a child is very sleepy, confused, or vomiting: Call 911. Keep packaging for clinicians. For guidance, call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 (U.S.).4–6

How kids get it. Vape/gas shops and online stores — often marketed as “legal hemp” with candy flavors and cartoon branding. Age checks can be weak or absent.1–3

Talk about it—script. “Even when it says ‘hemp,’ these products are strong drugs. Labels aren’t reliable, and people end up in the ER. I care about you — let’s make safer choices together.”

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: “Hemp products can’t get you high.”
Fact: Delta-8/Delta-10 are intoxicating THC analogs; ERs see hallucinations and psychosis cases.1–3,5

Myth: “If it’s on a shelf, it’s regulated.”
Fact: Many products bypass state cannabis rules; testing/labeling are inconsistent and potency varies widely.1–2
