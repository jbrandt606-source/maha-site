Kratom: What Parents Should Know

The main chemical in kratom, mitragynine, converts into 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH), a compound stronger than morphine. Research confirms opioid-like effects, dependence, and withdrawal. CDC data show hundreds of U.S. deaths annually involving kratom, often alongside fentanyl or alcohol. FDA warns against all kratom use.

Tianeptine: What Parents Should Know

Tianeptine acts on opioid receptors and has been linked to addiction, withdrawal, and overdose. It’s sold in products like ZaZa, Pegasus, and Neptune’s Fix. Poison centers report severe cases needing naloxone. FDA calls it a dangerous opioid disguised as a supplement.

Phenibut: What Parents Should Know

Phenibut is a synthetic tranquilizer acting on GABA receptors. It causes dependence and withdrawal like benzodiazepines. Poison centers report increasing hospitalizations from powders sold as “stress” or “sleep” aids. FDA says phenibut is not a lawful dietary ingredient.

THC Extracts: What Parents Should Know

Delta-8, Delta-10, and THC-O are synthetic THC variants sold in vape shops and gas stations. They mimic marijuana’s effects and have caused hallucinations and psychosis. THC-O is federally illegal; states are banning Delta-8 and Delta-10 due to rising teen use.

Hallucinogens: What Parents Should Know

Psychedelics such as psilocybin (“magic mushrooms”), LSD, and DMT are marketed online for “microdosing.” These can cause panic, psychosis, flashbacks, and impaired judgment. Synthetic hallucinogens like NBOMe have been linked to fatal overdoses.

Nitrous Oxide: What Parents Should Know

“Whippets” or nitrous cartridges cause brief euphoria but can result in brain injury, paralysis, or death. Chronic use destroys vitamin B12 and damages nerves. ER visits for nitrous-related injuries are increasing among teens and young adults.

Mad Honey: What Parents Should Know

Mad honey from rhododendron nectar contains grayanotoxins. Even small doses cause vomiting, low heart rate, and fainting. It’s sold online as “natural,” but can cause cardiac arrest. Call Poison Control if ingested.

Other Common Substances: What Parents Should Know

Gas-station “herbal highs” like kava drinks or energy shots may hide opioids, benzodiazepines, or stimulants. FDA and CDC warn these unregulated blends cause dependence, seizures, and hospitalizations. Treat any “energy” or “relaxation” shot as potentially addictive.
