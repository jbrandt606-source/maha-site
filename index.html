Kratom: What Parents Should Know

Acts like an opioid. Mitragynine converts to 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) in the body; human studies confirm measurable 7-OH after kratom use and animal work shows 7-OH can be several times stronger than morphine.

Real harms. CDC reports hundreds of kratom-positive overdose deaths across many states; poison centers logged 10k+ cases since 2014; doctors document dependence and neonatal withdrawal.

Tianeptine: What Parents Should Know

“ZaZa/Neptune’s Fix” products contain an opioid-acting drug linked to addiction and overdose. Many ER cases are reversed with naloxone. FDA has issued multiple national warnings; several states ban it.

Phenibut: What Parents Should Know

A synthetic tranquilizer (GABAergic). Causes dependence and withdrawal like benzodiazepines. Poison-center and ICU cases are rising; FDA says it’s not a lawful dietary ingredient.

THC Extracts: What Parents Should Know

Delta-8/Delta-10 gummies and vapes exploit hemp loopholes; pediatric exposures and ER visits reported. THC-O is federally illegal and may be even more potent than Delta-9 THC.

Hallucinogens: What Parents Should Know

Psilocybin/LSD/DMT marketed as “microdosing” wellness; risks include panic, psychosis, flashbacks (HPPD), and injuries from impaired judgment. Counterfeit tabs can contain NBOMe.

Nitrous Oxide: What Parents Should Know

“Whippets” can cause hypoxia, accidents, and with chronic use, B12 inactivation → neuropathy and spinal-cord injury. Teen clusters appear in poison-center data.

Mad Honey: What Parents Should Know

Grayanotoxins from rhododendron nectar → dizziness, vomiting, dangerous bradycardia/hypotension within minutes. No health benefits; call Poison Control after ingestion.

Other Common Substances: What Parents Should Know

Gas-station/online “herbal highs” (kava drinks, “focus” shots, kratom blends) often hide psychoactive drugs and use deceptive labels. FDA/CDC/poison centers warn of seizures, dependence, and hospitalizations.
