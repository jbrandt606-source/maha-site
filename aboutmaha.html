About MAHA • Mothers Against Herbal Abuse

“Are we supposed to make America healthy again — or make America high again?”
Our answer: teach families the facts and give communities the tools to protect kids.

MAHA exists to educate parents, schools, and neighborhoods about unregulated “herbal” drug products sold in gas stations, vape shops, and online — and to empower communities to remove them. We translate medical evidence into plain language, show what these products look like in stores, and provide ready-to-use letters, talking points, and action steps that work at the federal, state, and local levels.

What we do

  • Parent education: short, referenced guides that explain what the products are, how they harm kids, and what to watch for.
  • Community tools: form letters, meeting scripts, and store-owner one-pagers to start effective conversations.
  • Policy primers: simple “who does what” worksheets so families can ask the right agency to act.
  • Visual evidence: real-world packaging examples so caregivers can recognize candy-style marketing immediately.

How you can use MAHA

  • Learn: open any topic in the Information Center and share it with your school, church, or PTA.
  • Report: when you see risky products, use our reporting links to notify health agencies and consumer-protection teams.
  • Act: send a form letter to your city council, state lawmakers, or Congress; bring the handouts to local meetings.
  • Protect: ask retailers to remove child-appealing packs; request warning signs or age checks in your town.

Our mission

  • Educate every parent and caregiver with clear, evidence-based information.
  • Equip communities with practical tools to change policy and store practices.
  • Eliminate unregulated psychoactive products from places kids shop and play.

When families know what’s on the shelf — and how to respond — communities get healthier. Together we can make America healthy again, not high again.
