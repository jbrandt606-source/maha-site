About MAHA • Mothers Against Herbal Abuse

We’re here to educate families, equip communities, and empower parents to get dangerous “herbal” drug products out of the places kids shop and play.

“Are we supposed to make America healthy again — or make America high again?”
For us, the answer is clear: we choose health, knowledge, and community.

MAHA began with a simple observation: in too many neighborhoods, stores sell products that look like candy but act like drugs. They sit between the registers and the snack aisle, packaged as “natural” or “wellness” — and families are left to sort the truth from the marketing.

We decided to change that. MAHA translates medical evidence into plain language, shows what these products look like in real stores, and provides practical tools so parents and neighbors can protect their communities.

What We Believe

  • Every parent has power. You don’t need a title to make change — you just need to speak up.
  • Education saves lives. When we understand what’s being sold and why it’s dangerous, we can protect children.
  • Community is the antidote. Real safety comes when parents, schools, health leaders, and retailers work together.

What We Do

Parent education. Short, referenced guides that show what the products look like, explain the risks, and list what to watch for.

Community tools. Ready-to-use letters, meeting scripts, and one-page handouts for schools, councils, and retailers.

Policy primers. Clear “who does what” sheets so families can ask the right agency — FDA, DEA, FTC, or state boards — to act.

Visual evidence. Real-world packaging examples so caregivers can spot candy-style marketing immediately.

How to Use MAHA

  • Learn: Open any topic in the Information Center and share it with your PTA, faith group, or clinic.
  • Report: If you see risky products, use the reporting links on our Federal/State pages to notify agencies.
  • Act: Send a form letter to your council, state lawmakers, or Congress; bring handouts to local meetings.
  • Protect: Ask retailers to remove child-appealing packs; request warning signs or age checks in your town.

Our Mission

  • Educate families with clear, trustworthy information about unregulated “herbal” drugs.
  • Equip communities with practical tools to change store practices and policy.
  • Empower parents to take back their neighborhoods — one store, one conversation, one vote at a time.

When families are informed, communities are strong — and when communities are strong, change is unstoppable. We’re MAHA, and we’re here to make America healthy again — not high again.
