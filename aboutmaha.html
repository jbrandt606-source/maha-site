About MAHA • Mothers Against Herbal Abuse

We’re a grassroots movement of parents, healthcare professionals, and community members working to educate families, equip communities, and empower parents to get dangerous “herbal” drug products out of local stores and online marketplaces.

“Are we supposed to make America healthy again — or make America high again?”
For us, the answer is clear: we choose health, knowledge, and community.

MAHA began with a mother’s question — and the disbelief that in so many towns, there are now more gas stations selling “wellness highs” than selling groceries. Behind the neon lights and flashy labels, unregulated products are being sold as supplements, putting families and children at risk. These items aren’t harmless herbs — they’re often potent drugs hidden in plain sight.

MAHA was founded to change that. We believe that public health should come before profit and pseudo-science. We speak out where regulation has failed — against gas-station opioids, mislabeled “herbal” drinks, and deceptive supplement marketing that preys on young people. Our mission is to make sure every parent knows what’s on the shelf, what’s in it, and how to take action.

What We Believe

  • Every parent has power. You don’t need a title to make change — just a voice and the will to use it.
  • Education saves lives. When families understand what’s being sold and why it’s dangerous, they can protect their children.
  • Community is the antidote. Real safety comes when parents, schools, and leaders work together to demand accountability.

What We Do

Parent education. Short, referenced guides that show what the products look like, explain the risks, and outline what to watch for.

Community tools. Ready-to-use letters, meeting templates, and handouts for schools, councils, and retailers.

Policy primers. Clear, simple guides showing which agencies — FDA, DEA, FTC, or state boards — can take action.

Visual evidence. Real-world packaging examples so caregivers can recognize candy-style marketing instantly.

How to Use MAHA

  • Learn: Explore each topic in the Information Center and share it with your PTA, church, or community group.
  • Report: If you see risky products, use our reporting links to notify state or federal health authorities.
  • Act: Send a form letter to your city council, state legislature, or Congress; bring awareness materials to meetings.
  • Protect: Encourage retailers to remove dangerous or child-appealing products; promote warning signs and age checks.

Our Mission

  • Educate families with clear, trustworthy information about unregulated “herbal” drugs.
  • Equip communities with data and practical tools to influence policy and store practices.
  • Empower parents to take back their neighborhoods — one store, one meeting, one law at a time.

When families are informed, communities are strong — and when communities are strong, change is unstoppable.

We’re MAHA. And we’re here to make America healthy again — not high again.
