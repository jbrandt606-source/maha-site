Kratom: What Parents Should Know

Mitragynine converts into 7‑hydroxymitragynine (7‑OH), which binds the same opioid receptors as morphine. Human studies confirm measurable 7‑OH after kratom use; animal work shows 7‑OH can be several times stronger than morphine.

CDC reports hundreds of kratom‑positive overdose deaths across many states, with medical examiners often judging kratom causal. U.S. poison centers recorded 10,272 kratom cases (2014–2023), and clinicians document dependence, withdrawal, and neonatal abstinence.
