Hallucinogens: What Parents Should Know

Psychedelics are being rebranded as “wellness” or “microdose” products — but effects can include panic, psychosis, accidents, and long-lasting flashbacks.

Powerful effects on perception. Psilocybin (“magic mushrooms”), LSD, and DMT alter sensory processing and judgment; users may see/hear things that aren’t there and misjudge risk.1,4

ER presentations are rising. Emergency departments report cases of acute anxiety/psychosis, risky behavior, injuries, and self-harm after use — including from “microdose” products.6–7

Flashbacks can occur weeks later. HPPD (hallucinogen persisting perception disorder) can cause recurring visual disturbances even after stopping use.9

“Hemp shop” ≠ safety. Vape/smoke shops and online stores sell psychoactive “mushroom” and LSD-analog products outside medical oversight with inconsistent labeling/testing.1–3,11–14

Popular Hallucinogens & “Mushroom” Products in Retail

“Amanita” gummies/chocolates — contain muscimol/ibotenic acid (from Amanita muscaria), not psilocybin. Can cause confusion, ataxia, sleepiness, or agitation; potency varies.

“Mushroom” chocolate bars — branding suggests psilocybin; may be counterfeit or contain unknown actives. Labels are unreliable; risks include panic, vomiting, and injuries from impaired judgment.

LSD-analog tabs — 1P-LSD, AL-LAD, and other analogs sold online can mimic LSD; counterfeit tabs can contain NBOMe compounds linked to fatalities.13–14

DMT “vape pens” — highly potent, ultra-rapid effects; intoxication can cause panic, loss of coordination, and dangerous behavior in seconds.

Parent Playbook

What to watch for. Candy-style gummies, chocolate bars, or vapes with “mushroom,” “Amanita,” or “microdose” branding; perforated paper “tabs.” Signs include dilated pupils, anxiety/panic, confusion, poor balance, or visual distortions.1,4,6–7

If someone is in trouble. For severe agitation, hallucinations with unsafe behavior, injuries, chest pain, or persistent vomiting — call 911. Keep packaging for clinicians. For guidance, call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 (U.S.).

How kids get it. Vape/smoke shops, online sellers, and social media “microdose” marketing with wellness claims. Packaging often mimics snacks or supplements; age checks can be weak.

Talk about it — script. “Some ‘mushroom’ or ‘hemp’ products are strong hallucinogens. Labels aren’t reliable. I want you safe — let’s look at what doctors and the FDA say and make a plan together.”

Myths vs. Facts

Myth: “Microdosing is always safe.”
Fact: Evidence is limited and inconsistent; some users still experience panic, impaired judgment, or HPPD-type symptoms.8–9

Myth: “If it’s sold as ‘mushroom chocolate,’ it’s natural and mild.”
Fact: Products can contain concentrated psychoactives or LSD-type analogs; counterfeit tabs may include NBOMe linked to deaths.11–14
