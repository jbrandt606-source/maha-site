State – How to Take Action

States can stop sales quickly. Ask lawmakers and agencies to use every tool they have.

State Senate, House & Governor

What they can do
• Pass full bans on kratom, tianeptine, phenibut, “mushroom” edibles.
• Fund poison-control and public-health outreach.
• Give boards fast-track authority to add new drugs.

Limitations
• State law stops at the border; online/interstate sales persist.

Ask them to
• Enact bans and close loopholes (supplement/food additive).
• Resource enforcement and school education.

State Health Department

What they can do
• Issue HAN-style alerts; publish dashboards on ER visits/poisonings.
• Use emergency powers to curb retail sales temporarily.

Limitations
• Warns and restricts; does not prosecute.

Ask them to
• Release frequent alerts; partner with schools & local BOHs.
• Share real-time data with policymakers and media.

State Board of Pharmacy

What they can do
• Add substances to the state controlled list.
• Stop licensed pharmacies from selling risky products.

Limitations
• Limited power over unlicensed retailers (vape/gas shops).

Ask them to
• Schedule these substances now by rule.
• Recommend full legislative bans for non-pharmacy sellers.

State Medical Associations

What they can do
• Educate clinicians and the public; publish guidance & statements.
• Testify to committees with patient cases and data.

Limitations
• Influence policy but don’t enforce it.

Ask them to
• Endorse bans; warn against kratom/tianeptine use.
• Share de-identified cases of harm.
